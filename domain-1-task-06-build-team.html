📖 Tuckman's Team Development Model

🇺🇸 English

Bruce Tuckman (1965) identified 5 stages of team development. Understanding these stages helps PM anticipate team behavior and respond appropriately. Teams can move backward through stages when members change or circumstances shift significantly.

PMI — Tuckman Team Development →

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Bruce Tuckman (1965) xác định 5 giai đoạn phát triển nhóm. Hiểu các giai đoạn này giúp PM dự đoán hành vi nhóm và phản hồi phù hợp. Các nhóm có thể quay lại giai đoạn trước khi thành viên thay đổi hoặc hoàn cảnh thay đổi đáng kể.

Forming

Storming
Xung đột		 Conflict, resistance, power struggles, frustration; some may leave Coach, mediate, reinforce team charter; normalize conflict as healthy Sprint 2-3; retros surface real issues
Norming
Chuẩn hóa		 Trust builds, collaboration improves, agreed ways of working Reinforce positive behaviors, delegate, celebrate small wins Consistent sprint velocity; DoD followed

Hiệu suất cao		 High productivity, interdependent, motivated, self-managing Empower, protect from interference, recognize achievements Flow state; high velocity; low defects

Giải tán		 Project ends, sadness or relief, transition out Celebrate, lessons learned, knowledge transfer, recognition Project closure sprint; knowledge base handoff
🎯
Exam Tips — Build a Team
  • Storming = normal, not a failure. PM should facilitate, not suppress conflict
  • Teams can REGRESS — when new member joins, or major disruption, team may return to Forming/Storming
  • PM goal: reach and MAINTAIN Performing stage as long as possible
  • Recognition: PM should recognize team AND individual achievements; team recognition builds cohesion
  • Diversity: diverse teams perform better but take longer to norm — PM must facilitate inclusion

🎗️ Recognition & Rewards / Ghi nhận và Khen thưởng

🇺🇸 English

Recognition is a powerful motivator. Key principles:

  • Timely: Recognize SOON after the achievement
  • Specific: Name what was done and why it mattered
  • Appropriate: Match recognition to the person's preference (public vs private)
  • Team vs Individual: Balance both — team celebrates collective wins; individual recognition for standout contribution

In Agile: Sprint reviews are natural recognition moments. Retrospectives acknowledge team wins.

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Ghi nhận là động lực mạnh mẽ. Nguyên tắc chính:

  • Kịp thời: Ghi nhận NGAY sau thành tích
  • Cụ thể: Nêu rõ điều gì đã được làm và tại sao nó quan trọng
  • Phù hợp: Khớp với sở thích của người đó (công khai vs riêng tư)
  • Nhóm vs Cá nhân: Cân bằng cả hai

💼 Thực chiến / Scenario

🏢

FinTech Company X — New Cross-cultural Team

Tình huống: Project Alpha team gồm senior devs VN và junior devs PH vừa được assembled. Tuần đầu tiên: lịch sự, ít nói, mọi người chờ ai đó lead — đây là Forming stage.

PM actions per stage:

Forming: Tổ chức team kickoff với team charter workshop. Clarify roles, goals, working agreements. Introduce team to each other beyond just names — background, strengths, interests. Create Slack channel #alpha-team-intros.

Storming (Sprint 2-3): VN seniors và PH juniors có tension về code review expectations. PM tổ chức retrospective sớm, normalize conflict, set clear code review guidelines. Coach VN seniors về constructive feedback (không harsh với juniors).

Norming (Sprint 4+): Team establish DoD together, velocity stabilizes, pair programming becomes natural. PM celebrates: shoutout in all-hands for team completing first partner API integration.

✏️ Practice Questions

Question 1
A newly assembled project team is experiencing frequent disagreements and some members are resistant to team processes. Which stage of team development is this, and what should the PM do?
  • A. Forming — clarify roles and goals
  • B. Storming — coach through conflicts and reinforce team norms
  • C. Norming — celebrate team achievements
  • D. Performing — give team full autonomy
✅ Answer: B — Frequent disagreements and resistance indicate Storming stage. PM should coach through conflicts, help the team establish norms, and reinforce the team charter. This is normal — the PM's job is to facilitate through it, not panic.
Question 2
A previously high-performing team has recently experienced two departures and has three new members. What team development stage are they MOST likely in?
  • A. Performing — they have experienced members
  • B. Adjourning
  • C. Back to Forming or Storming — team composition changed significantly
  • D. Norming
✅ Answer: C — Tuckman's model explicitly states that teams can regress when membership changes significantly. Adding three new members to a five-to-seven person team is a major disruption — new relationships must form, norms must be re-established, and trust rebuilt. The team must re-enter Forming (getting to know each other) or Storming (asserting new dynamics) even if they were previously Performing.
Question 3
The PM notices team members are starting to challenge each other's ideas in sprint planning, causing tension. This is MOST consistent with which Tuckman stage?
  • A. Forming
  • B. Storming
  • C. Norming
  • D. Performing
✅ Answer: B — Storming is characterized by conflict as team members assert their individual perspectives, challenge ideas, and compete for influence. This is a healthy and necessary stage — the PM should not suppress it. The correct response is to coach through it: acknowledge the tension, reinforce psychological safety, and help the team work toward agreed norms (which leads to Norming).
