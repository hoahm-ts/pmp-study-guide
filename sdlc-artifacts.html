🌊 Waterfall / Predictive SDLC

🇺🇸 English

Waterfall follows sequential phases with formal gates between each. Every phase produces documented artifacts that become the baseline for the next. Change is controlled and formal.

Best for: Fixed-scope projects with clear requirements, regulatory-heavy environments, infrastructure/construction, large enterprise programs.

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Waterfall theo các giai đoạn tuần tự với cổng kiểm soát chính thức giữa mỗi giai đoạn. Mỗi giai đoạn tạo ra tài liệu trở thành baseline cho giai đoạn tiếp theo.

Phù hợp với: Dự án phạm vi cố định, yêu cầu rõ ràng, môi trường tuân thủ cao, hạ tầng, chương trình doanh nghiệp lớn.

📌
Waterfall — Key PM Rule

Every phase produces a baseline. Changes to the baseline require formal CCB approval. Never skip the gate review — it's the only mechanism that keeps scope, cost, and schedule under control. EVM is your early warning system during execution.

🏃 Scrum SDLC

🇺🇸 English

Scrum delivers in fixed-length iterations (Sprints, typically 2 weeks). The Product Owner defines what gets built; the team decides how. Transparency through artifacts and ceremonies is the core control mechanism.

Best for: Product development with evolving requirements, digital products, startups, cross-functional teams with good collaboration, when fast feedback loops matter.

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Scrum deliver theo các vòng lặp cố định (Sprints, thường 2 tuần). Product Owner định nghĩa cái gì cần xây dựng; team quyết định cách thực hiện.

Phù hợp với: Phát triển sản phẩm với yêu cầu thay đổi, sản phẩm kỹ thuật số, startup, team cross-functional tốt, khi feedback loop nhanh quan trọng.

📌
Scrum — Key PM Rule

Velocity is a forecasting tool, not a performance metric. Never use velocity to compare teams or pressure individuals. The sprint burndown tells you if the sprint goal is at risk — the velocity chart tells you if the team's capacity is stable over time. Both are needed.

📋 Kanban SDLC

🇺🇸 English

Kanban is a continuous flow system — no sprints, no iterations. Work is pulled when capacity is available. Control comes from WIP limits, explicit policies, and flow metrics rather than planning ceremonies.

Best for: Operations/support teams, maintenance work, teams with highly variable request types, bug-fix queues, service desks, teams not ready for full Scrum ceremonies.

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Kanban là hệ thống luồng liên tục — không có sprints. Công việc được kéo khi có dung lượng. Kiểm soát đến từ giới hạn WIP, chính sách rõ ràng và số liệu luồng.

Phù hợp với: Team vận hành/hỗ trợ, bảo trì, yêu cầu biến thiên cao, hàng đợi sửa lỗi, service desk.

Board ElementWhat to DefineExample
ColumnsEach column = a stage of work. Define entry and exit criteria for each.Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → Testing → Done
WIP LimitsMaximum items allowed in each active column. Violations = system signal.In Progress: max 3 | In Review: max 2 | Testing: max 4
Work Types / Classes of ServiceDifferent work types get different priority treatment and SLAs.Expedite (P0) / Fixed Date / Standard / Intangible (tech debt)
Pull PoliciesWhen can an item be pulled to the next stage? Who can pull?Story must have AC written and approved before entering "In Progress"
Done DefinitionWhat does "Done" mean? Same as DoD in Scrum.Code reviewed, tests passing, deployed to staging, PO accepted
Meeting CadenceUnlike Scrum, meetings are triggered by need or a light cadence.Daily standup (15 min) + weekly replenishment + monthly retrospective

Key Charts & Dashboards

ChartWhat It ShowsHow to Use It
Cumulative Flow Diagram (CFD) Volume of work in each column over time. Bands widen = WIP increasing. Flat band = bottleneck. Monitor daily. Widening "In Progress" band = team overloaded or blocked.
Lead Time Distribution How long items take from creation to done. Histogram shows spread and outliers. Use p85 (85th percentile) as your SLA commitment — not average.
Cycle Time Scatterplot Each dot = one work item. X = date completed, Y = days to complete. Shows outliers and patterns. Outliers above the p85 line need investigation — what made them slow?
Throughput Chart Items completed per week over time. Shows team capacity trend. Use for forecasting: "at X items/week, when will the backlog be done?"
Aging WIP Chart Items currently on board, sorted by how long they've been in the current stage. Items above p85 threshold = at risk. Escalate or investigate.
Monte Carlo Forecast Probabilistic forecast of when backlog will be complete, based on throughput history. "85% probability of completing 30 items by [date]." Use for stakeholder commitments.

📌
Kanban — Key PM Rule

Stop starting, start finishing. WIP limits exist for a reason — a team that violates them constantly is telling you something about their process, not their discipline. Use the CFD and Aging WIP chart as your daily early warning system, not as reports you generate after the fact.

🔀 Hybrid SDLC

🇺🇸 English

Hybrid combines predictive governance (milestones, formal approvals, change control) with agile delivery (sprints, backlog, iterative releases). Common in enterprises that need executive reporting but want team-level agility.

Best for: Regulated industries with fixed compliance gates, large programs with multiple delivery teams, projects with some fixed scope and some evolving features.

🇻🇳 Tiếng Việt

Hybrid kết hợp quản trị predictive (milestones, phê duyệt chính thức, change control) với delivery agile (sprints, backlog, release lặp lại). Phổ biến trong doanh nghiệp cần báo cáo cấp cao nhưng muốn agility ở cấp team.

Hybrid — Key PM Rule

The PM's job in a hybrid model is to translate between layers — convert sprint velocity into milestone forecast language for executives, and convert compliance/regulatory requirements into backlog items the team can implement. Never let the two layers drift apart without a clear mapping.

🤖 AI Tools for SDLC Artifact Management

🤖
How AI Augments SDLC Artifact Work

AI dramatically reduces the time to create, review, and maintain SDLC artifacts. Use it to generate first drafts, audit completeness, convert between methodology formats, and translate team data into executive-ready dashboards.

Sample Claude Prompts

Waterfall — Generate a WBS from project description You are a senior PM. Help me create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) for my project. Project: [name and brief description] Key deliverables: [list the main outputs] Team: [roles involved] Duration: [months] Create a WBS with: 1. Level 1: Major deliverable categories (5-7 max) 2. Level 2: Work packages under each category (3-6 per L1) 3. Level 3: Tasks under each work package (for complex items only) Rules: - Every node must be a noun (deliverable), not a verb (activity) - 100% rule: WBS must include 100% of project scope - No overlap between branches - Work packages should be estimable (3-10 days each) Also flag: which work packages are on the critical path (highest risk if delayed)?
Scrum — Generate sprint planning inputs from backlog Help me prepare for sprint planning. Sprint: [Sprint N] | Duration: [2 weeks] | Team capacity: [SP available — account for leave, ceremonies] Product Backlog (top items, with story points): [Story 1] — [SP] — Priority: [High/Med/Low] — AC: [brief] [Story 2] — [SP] — Priority: [...] [add more...] Sprint goal direction from PO: [what the PO wants to achieve this sprint] Previous sprint velocity: [SP] | Unplanned work typical %: [%] Generate: 1. Recommended sprint scope (which stories to pull, total SP) 2. Sprint Goal statement (1 sentence, outcome-focused) 3. Risk flags (stories with unclear AC, external dependencies, technical unknowns) 4. Stories to split (any > 8 SP should be broken down) 5. Capacity check: does committed scope fit within team capacity?
Kanban — Interpret flow metrics and diagnose bottleneck Analyze my Kanban flow metrics and diagnose what's happening. Team: [size, roles] Period: [last 4 weeks] Flow data: Throughput (items/week): Week1:[n] Week2:[n] Week3:[n] Week4:[n] Avg cycle time: [days] | p85 cycle time: [days] | SLA target: [days] WIP at each stage (current): Backlog:[n] Ready:[n] InProgress:[n] Review:[n] Testing:[n] Done:[n] WIP violations this week: [count] Items blocked: [count] | Oldest blocked item: [days] Items past SLA: [count out of total done] CFD observation: [describe what you see — widening band? flat area? sudden drop?] Analyze: 1. Where is the bottleneck? (which column is the constraint?) 2. Is throughput stable, improving, or declining? Root cause? 3. Is cycle time within SLA? What's the main driver of slow items? 4. What WIP limit should I adjust? 5. Top 3 actions to improve flow next week
